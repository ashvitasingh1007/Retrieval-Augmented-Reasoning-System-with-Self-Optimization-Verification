Fusion energy is a process where two light atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus, releasing a large amount of energy. It powers the sun and stars.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a method that combines retrieval of relevant documents with language model generation to produce grounded and factual answers.

A vector database stores data as embeddings, which are numerical representations of text. These embeddings allow semantic search instead of keyword matching.

FAISS is a library developed by Facebook AI that allows efficient similarity search over large collections of vectors.

Hallucination in language models refers to generating incorrect or unsupported information that is not grounded in the provided data.

Evaluation of RAG systems includes metrics such as faithfulness, relevance, and answer correctness.

Agentic AI refers to systems that can plan, act, and iterate toward a goal autonomously.

Verification in RAG systems involves checking whether generated answers are supported by retrieved documents.
